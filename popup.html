Some food for thought to put this in historical context: Having now won the WS twice in this decade while the Yankees have not won a WS at all in this decade, the Red Sox are assured of ending the 2010s with more WS titles in a decade than the Yankees for the first time since the 1910s (when the Red Sox won 4 and the Yankees won zero). The Red Sox and Yankees each won 2 WS titles in the decade from 2000 to 2009. If neither the Yankees nor the Mets win the WS in 2019, that will make the 2010s the first decade since the 1910s in which no New York City baseball team won the WS at all. The New York Giants lost the WS four times in the 1910s, and the Brooklyn Robins once. The Yankees didn't appear in a WS until 1921. By all accounts, the Steinbrenner sons are relative penny pinchers who wish to avoid the overly lavish spending that characterized their father's ownership of the Yankees until he died in 2010. The Yankees managed to avoid paying the luxury tax this year for the first time in a long while. But the Yankees won 7 WS titles during George Steinbrenner's 37 years as owner. Championships aren't free and Billy "Moneyball" Beane has never won a WS title as a front office executive. Yankee fans, from an outsider's perspective you're getting shortchanged by the team's owners.

All is joyful in Red Sox Nation, once again. Many thanks to both the Sox and Dodgers for a superb, if short-lived, World Series. It might have been Brasier who set the tone for the Sox onslaught, yelling "Get back in the box!" to the Yankees' dithering Luke Voit. Not only have the Sox forever put to rest the curse of the Bambino, but David Price has vanquished his own playoff demons. The only question left is if manager Alex Cora and his richly diverse, multi-colored team will go to the White House, which remains blackened by our cursedly nativist president.
